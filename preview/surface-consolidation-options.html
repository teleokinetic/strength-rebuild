Too many cooks — three consolidations

Today a session lives on three surfaces: the Route (map), the Clock (moment-to-moment loop), and the Sheet (the original all-inputs grid). Three ways to thin the kitchen. Screens shown in the light theme, real Day B content, mid-session. Static proposal — nothing wired. History, charts, and the finish flow are untouched in all three.

Option 1
The route absorbs the sheet

Stops expand in place into the sheet's edit grid — every set, load, reps, RIR, ± set, note — then fold away. The sheet retires. Two surfaces, one job each: Route = plan, fix, see where you stand. Clock = do the next set.

Option 3
Demote the sheet

Keep all three, but give the kitchen one door: the route is the only hub. Stops jump to the clock (as shipped); a small edit opens the old sheet scrolled to that movement. "Back to sets" leaves the clock; "Set sheet" leaves the route's footer.

Recommendation

Option 1 — the route absorbs the sheet. It's the only one that actually reduces the surface count while keeping every capability: the rail stays the calm map you glance at from the bench, and the grid appears exactly where your thumb already is when something needs fixing. Your gym instinct in the screenshots — tapping the stop — is the same gesture this design builds on. Option 3 is the safe fallback if retiring the sheet feels premature; Option 2 trades away the calm that makes the route worth having.

Build note for Option 1: the sheet's code (grids, ± set, notes) moves into the expanded stop nearly verbatim; #/session retires; finish flow and history untouched. Nothing here is wired — pick a direction and the next session builds it.
